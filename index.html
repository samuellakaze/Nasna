The Challenge

Problem Statement

Unemployment persists in Rwanda's informal sector, particularly affecting the youth in both urban and rural areas. According to the National Institute of Statistics of Rwanda, the youth unemployment rate in 2025 ranges between 15.4% and 21%. Limited access to employment opportunities and low productivity contribute to continuous underemployment and economic vulnerability. Addressing this issue is critical to promoting inclusive economic growth by 2030.

Why We Exist

Our Mission

Our mission is to address youth unemployment in Rwanda's informal sector by generating research-driven strategies that enhance productivity, expand employment opportunities, and promote inclusive economic development by 2030.

What Guides Us

Our Values

As Nasna, we are purpose-driven, curious, and deeply committed to shaping Africa's future. We lead with integrity in everything we do, holding ourselves accountable to the highest standards. We embrace collaboration, knowing that our diverse backgrounds and perspectives make us stronger together. We champion innovation as a tool for change, constantly seeking creative solutions to the challenges our continent faces. And above all, we are grounded in a spirit of service — believing that true leadership is about uplifting others and leaving a lasting impact on the communities we serve.
